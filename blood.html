    Most blood types fall into one of four blood type groups, according to whether they contain A or B antigens.
    For example, people with
    A antigens have type A blood, while those with B antigens have type B blood.
    People with both A and B antigens have type AB blood, while
    people with neither antigen on their red blood cells have type O blood.
